Frequently Asked Questions

How long should a password be?

At least 16 characters. The longer the better — length is one of the most important factors in password strength.

Should I reuse passwords?

Never. If one account is breached, reusing passwords means all your other accounts become vulnerable too.

Are the generated passwords stored anywhere?

No. All passwords are generated locally in your browser and are never sent to or stored on any server.

What is two-factor authentication?

Two-factor authentication (2FA) adds a second verification step beyond your password, such as a code sent to your phone. It significantly increases account security.

What is a password manager?

A password manager is an app that securely stores all your passwords in one place, encrypted behind a single master password. It removes the need to memorize every password.

How often should I change my password?

You only need to change a password if you suspect it has been compromised or if a service you use has been breached. Frequent changes without reason are no longer recommended by security experts.

What makes a password weak?

Short length, common words, predictable patterns like "123456" or "password", and reusing passwords across multiple sites all make a password weak.

Is it safe to use a password generator?

Yes. This generator runs entirely in your browser meaning no passwords are ever transmitted over the internet or saved anywhere.

What is a data breach?

A data breach is when unauthorized individuals gain access to private data, such as usernames and passwords stored by a company. Breached credentials are often sold or used to attempt logins on other sites.

What is phishing and how do I avoid it?

Phishing is when attackers trick you into revealing your credentials by posing as a legitimate service. Always double check the URL before entering a password and never click suspicious links in emails.

Should I use my browser's built-in password manager?

It's better than nothing but a dedicated password manager like Bitwarden or 1Password offers stronger encryption, cross-device syncing, and better security overall.

What is encryption?

Encryption is the process of converting data into an unreadable format that can only be decoded with the correct key. Good password managers use strong encryption to protect your stored passwords.
